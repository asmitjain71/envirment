Turning Photosynthesis into Physical Carbon Storage

59 billion tonnes.

That’s how much carbon we must cut from the global economy while un-cooking the planet.

Cutting emissions alone won’t get us there. Most carbon removal technologies are too energy-intensive, too expensive, and too slow to scale. They fight physics with infrastructure—and lose on timelines. We take a different path.

Plants already capture carbon efficiently at planetary scale. We make that carbon permanent by stopping decomposition—drying biomass to eliminate microbes and sealing it from water and gases. Hermatic locks carbon away in a way that is durable, affordable, and deployable now.

Efficient & Sustainable

Most carbon removal approaches rely on energy-intensive processes that either extract CO₂ from the air or thermally convert biomass (pyrolysis) releasing a portion of carbon back into the atmosphere and adding infrastructure overhead. These methods trade durability for energy and scale poorly under real-world constraints.

Hermatic takes a different approach. By stabilizing biomass directly without high-temperature conversion, we preserve more carbon per ton while using minimal energy. This enables durable, low-cost carbon storage that can be deployed wherever biomass already exists.

Carbon Vaulting

Hermatic repurposes biomass waste into dense, sealed carbon blocks for monitored underground storage. By combining natural photosynthesis with simple, low-energy engineering, it enables scalable, cost-effective carbon removal at global scale.

Land Restoration

Transform degraded or unused land into long-term carbon storage sites, then return the surface for productive use, enabling agriculture, solar generation, or other development while restoring value to the land.

About Hermatic

Hermatic is a deeptech climate infrastructure company building durable biological carbon storage to turn barren and underutilized land into long-term carbon sinks. Starting in Rajasthan, we deploy low-energy biomass storage systems that lock away carbon while restoring surface land for reforestation, agriculture, or solar use. Grounded in engineering and rigorous monitoring, and driven by the belief that climate stability requires bold infrastructure, Hermatic’s mission is to remove 5 million metric tons of CO₂ by 2030 while improving land and livelihoods.
